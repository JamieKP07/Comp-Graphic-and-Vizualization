# Comp-Graphic-and-Vizualization
Final Project Reflection
How do I approach designing software?

When I approach designing software, I start by breaking down the overall goal into smaller, manageable parts. For this project, I focused on identifying the main objects I wanted in my 3D scene, like the dice tower, dice, book, and potion bottle, and then thought about how each one could be built using basic shapes. Instead of trying to design everything at once, I approached it piece by piece, making sure each component worked before combining them into a full scene.

What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

This project helped me develop the ability to translate real-world or conceptual objects into simple geometric forms. I also improved my spatial awareness when designing layouts, especially when it came to positioning and scaling objects so they fit together naturally. Another skill I developed was thinking about how visual elements like lighting and textures contribute to the overall design, rather than just focusing on the shapes themselves.

What design process did you follow for your project work?

My design process was iterative and incremental. I started with a basic structure, like the main tower, and then gradually added additional objects and details. As I built each part, I adjusted transformations like scale, rotation, and position to improve how everything fit together. I also revisited earlier parts of the design multiple times to refine alignment and consistency as the scene became more complex.

How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

The approach of breaking down complex ideas into smaller components can be applied to any type of software project. Whether working on graphics, applications, or systems, focusing on one piece at a time makes problems easier to solve and reduces errors. Iterating on designs and continuously refining them is also a valuable habit that can improve both efficiency and final quality in future work.

How do I approach developing programs?

When developing programs, I focus on building functionality step by step and testing as I go. I try to avoid writing too much code at once without verifying it works, since that can make debugging more difficult. I also prioritize keeping my code organized so that changes are easier to make later.

What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One new strategy I used was organizing transformations more consistently, making sure that scale, rotation, and position for each object were grouped together. This made it easier to adjust objects without losing track of their properties. I also became more comfortable working with multiple components like SceneManager and ShaderManager and understanding how they interact to render the final scene.

How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration played a major role in my development process. Many parts of the scene, especially object placement, required repeated adjustments to get them aligned correctly. I would make small changes, test the result, and then refine further. This trial-and-error process helped me gradually improve the overall quality of the scene.

How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?

At the beginning of the project, my approach was more experimental and less structured. As I progressed through the milestones, I became more intentional with how I wrote and organized my code. I started planning ahead more, grouping related logic together, and making adjustments in a more controlled way. This made the later stages of the project smoother and easier to manage.

How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

Computer science provides problem-solving skills and technical knowledge that can be applied to many different fields. It teaches logical thinking, persistence, and the ability to break down complex problems, which are valuable in both professional and personal goals.

How do computational graphics and visualizations help in your educational pathway?

Computational graphics and visualizations help build a deeper understanding of how systems represent and process visual information. These skills can support future coursework in areas like game development, simulation, and user interface design. They also reinforce concepts like mathematics and spatial reasoning, which are important in many areas of computer science.

How do computational graphics and visualizations help in your professional pathway?

In a professional setting, these skills can be applied to fields such as game development, 3D modeling, simulation, and visualization tools. Even outside of graphics-specific roles, the experience gained from this project—such as problem solving, iterative development, and attention to detail—can transfer to general software development. Understanding how to build and manipulate visual environments can also be valuable in creating more engaging and interactive applications.
